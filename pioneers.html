Pioneers



Lakhmi Chand

The other most important Akhara in Haryana was the Pt. Lakhmi Chand. He is synonymous to Sang and Ragani is Haryana. He was born in 1903, in village Janti, Sonepat in a poor middle class peasant family. From an early age he stayed at his maternal uncle’s house and had chance to attend sangs of Pt. Deep Chand , and bhajans of Pt. Man Singh, Man Singh was a blind person and used to visit villages on his horse reciting his poems. Lakhmi Chand used to go with him to different villages and became a huge fan of his compositions and started to learn the art from Pt. Man Singh and became his disciple. In his lifetime Pt. Lakhmi Chand had joined with many other prominent artists such as Pt. Sarup Chand, Sohan Kundalwala, Pt. Net Ram . He was influenced by Pt. Net Ram and his sangs were based on Puranic themes. In 1930 he raised his independent troupe and excelled this art to perfection. In addition to adopting themes related to love, truth, dedicationand puranic mythical tradition, the use of folk language in his composition was exceptional. Almost all his sangs reflects social structure, religions, ethos of Haryana. His main sangs were Cheer Parva, Gopi Chand Bharathari, Hoor Maenka, Harish Chander, Jyani Chor, Nautanki, Pooranjan, Raja Bhoj, Puran Mal, Tara Chand, Veerat Parva. Pt. Lakhmi Chand had contributed a lot in the development of schools, temples, roads, chaupals, wells etc.



Baje Bhagat

Baje Bhagat was another prominent name within the Deep Chand Akhara in Haryana. He was born in 1902 in Sisana, Rohtak, he did not acquired any education from school like many other artists but he learned hindi and sanskrit from local teachers and was interested in poetry, music, dharma from his childhood. He met with Hardeva Swami through his father and adopted Hardeva Swami as his guru in 1921. He learned dancing, singing, acting from his guru. After the death of Hardeva Swami in 1926 he took the responsibilty of the troupe and began to perform with the troupe and excelled it. He earned donations for temples, wells, schools, chaupals, gaushalas by performing sangs in villages and gathering huge crowds. He has fans in Delhi, U.P, Rajasthan, he also staged his sangs in Lahore and and Multan. Some of his well known sangs were Chand Kiran, Harish chander, Jamal, Padmavat, Parun Mal, Raghubir, Roop Basant, Sarwar Neer etc.



Pt. Mange Ram

Born on March 1, 1906 in Rohtak Pt. Mange Ram was the most talented disciple of Lakhmi Chand Akhara. He belonged to a prosperous peasant family. From his early childhood he was a fan of Pt. lakhmi Chand and used to attend his sangs. He used to transport Lakhmi Chand's troupe in his taxi from one place from another for their preformances, during this time he learned a lot about singing and dancing by oberserving the performers. He joined the Lakhmi Chand troupe as a dancer and later started to take part in his sangs and became a regular member of his troupe. Later after about an year he started his own troupe along with Pt. Mai Chand. Mange Ram composed about 40 sangs from 1935 till his death in Garmukteshwar in 1967. His compositions were simple and easy to grasp for the local masses, he advocated to follow the high ideals and values of Vedic and Puranic times. His description of social, cultural and political life of contemporary Haryana was very close to reality. His performances attracted large crowds due to his new innovations in this art, he was the first artist to compose Ragani on political matters. Some of his popular compositions were Rajbala, Amar singh Rathor, Gautam Budh, Bhagat Singh, Dhruva Bhagat, Gopi Chand Maharaj, Hansbala-Rajpal, Krishnan Janam, Nav Rattan etc.



Dhanpat Singh

Dhanpat Singh was born in 1914 in Rohtak district and was also a Mirasi by caste and had inherited the art of singing and dancing from his family. He studied until 7th standard but was not much very fond of it, instead he used to go to visit sang performances rather than studying. At the age of 15 he became a disciple of Jamua Mir and had started to perform sangs. After acquiring all the talents of the art he started hisa own troupe in 1937, he was very much influenced by contemporary cinema and was the first artist to bring filmy tunes in his sangs and made it a common feature of his sangs. His themes included Hindu-Muslim unity, peasantry and some of his popular sangs were Amb Raja, Ban Debi, Jangal ki Rani, Roop Basant, Lilo-Chaman, Sheelo-Ashok etc. He was the most richest sangi of all of his contemporaries and contributed a huge sums of money for public welfare works.



Pt. Ram Krishnan Vyas

Ram Krishnan Vyas was born in 1925 at Hissar, Haryana, he started his formal education at school upto 10th standard and received training from Pt. Mai Ram Shastri. Many of sangs were composed by his gurus and the main themes of his compositions were puranic and love related themes. Like Dhanpat Singh his sangs were also based on filmy style and tunes. Some of his popular sangs were Dharam ki Geet, Bharat Hari, Shashi Kala, Sukhbir, Sarande, Satywan-Savitri etc.
